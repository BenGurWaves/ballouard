01 — The Awakening

1283

They did not tell time.
They announced it.

The first mechanical clocks emerge in European monasteries. The verge escapement is born — the first mechanism to divide eternity into measurable fractions.

02 — The Spiral

1675

Time becomes
personal.

Huygens introduces the balance spring. Timepieces shrink from architectural monoliths to pocket companions. Accuracy leaps from hours to minutes.

03 — The Whirlwind

1801

A mechanical answer
to gravity itself.

Abraham-Louis Breguet patents the tourbillon. He places the entire escapement inside a rotating cage. Patent No. 157.

04 — The Heartbeat

A heartbeat older than electricity, more precise than human perception. Four hertz. The frequency of perfection.
